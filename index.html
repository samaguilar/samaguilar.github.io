Humble Roots

Designer

The World's Produce. Delivered.

For our Entrepreneurial Process course, my team of about 7 students was tasked with developing and ultimately pitching a unique venture. Our interest and familiarity with local farmers in the state of Providence made it easier to develop some sort of solution that would help these local farmers who struggled to both sell their produce, and market themselves. I coined the name Humble Roots, after hearing that most of these local farms were family owned and relatively small. Our working slogan, ’The Worlds Produce. Delivered’ is a nod to our promise of developing a vast distribution network that connects customers to locally sourced produce and the people behind it.

UX Design & Development

Worked with 4 other students to redesign the most widely used course review aggregator on campus. Developed mockups, conducted preliminary research on how users interacted with information on previous website, and designed certain pages using HTML, CSS & JS. The redesigned site is now being used by over 6000+ Brown University students.

Birch Aquarium Sign

As part of my Animal Behavior Lab, I spent over 8 weeks studying Leopard Sharks at the Birch Aquarium in La Jolla, California. After researching both animal and guest behavior, I found a great opportunity to reevaluate the effectiveness of signage and deliver an improved version of the signs currently on display at the Leopard Shark exhibit.

Create unique music visualizations; all inside of MATLAB.

As our final project for Introduction to Programming, a couple of friends and I decided we build something that wasn't meant to run on MATLAB. The idea? A music visualizer that would take any song, and play it back with a cool visual moving to the frequency of the song. MLStudio offers over 8 unique visualizations including ColorBoom™, which matches the current "mood" of the song to a particular color.

True Cost of a Burger
